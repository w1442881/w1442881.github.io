Why this site?

This site was designed to meet the "portfolio" requirement for my Engineering Design 328 class at Los Rios Community College.

The idea for this site had been brewing in my head for a while. I've always had an interest in information design, but did not have the opportunity to explore technical site navigation through graphs, or to sort my own content in a way that was actually useful to me.

I got the opportunity to view some of the sample portfolios for other classes put together by some very talented engineering design students at Los Rios. Despite the impressive work they had done, I felt skeptical about the purported goal of the portfolio as a job-hunting aid. It seems to me like I have generated, then abandoned, a lot of content in my time, and didn't feel that showcasing my talents for a nonexistent potential employer was reason enough to spend all that effort. I feel at this stage in my life that I've come to terms with my actual needs, which revolve around deemphasizing things I don't like while spending more time on things I truly enjoy.

Hence the dream: what if I abandoned the concept of building a traditional portfolio or of designing a hierarchical web site and instead allowed the content I placed there to grow organically, as I was creating it? And, what if I put content there I actually could actually look up, reference, reuse and even grow, rather than focusing on showcasing unremarkable talents? This would give me the opportunity to work on things that were truly remarkable.

I haven't yet achieved any of these goals, and this site is not quite the realization of that dream, given the technical difficulties which I've uncovered while working on it. But it does point the way at achieving just that, over time, and with completely free tools. Parts of this site have been designed in such a way, and the result has so far been very encouraging, but all of it with the caveat that my own time is neither cheap nor plentiful.
